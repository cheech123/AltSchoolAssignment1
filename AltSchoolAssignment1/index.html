About Me

Hello, my name is Chizaram Daberechi Chukwu. My Alt School ID is ALT/SOE/023/2060.

I am 23 years old. I come from a family of 5; the last born and only girl with 2 brothers.

I am from Abia State, Nigeria. I have been to my state only twice, and that was when I was much younger. I would like to go more often, but there are existing circumstances beyond my control (insecurity issues).

I am a Computer Science student at Baze University, although I am about to graduate by God's grace.

I enjoy listening to music, watching shows and anime, reading, playing games.

From a very early age, I fell in love with computers. I've always been fascinated with how they work, how they "obey commands".

I've always wanted to learn how to write commands for the computer to accept.

My brother was the one who put me on to Alt School Africa. I looked it up and saw the amazing opportunities that could arise from being enrolled in the school.

I joined Alt School while still enrolled in the university because I realize my need for more in-depth knowledge and guidance in programming and how to navigate the tech world.

So far, I have only had pretty basic tech knowledge in the university.

I think keeping up with different courses and assignments and tests and deadlines have made it quite challenging for me to focus on expanding my knowledge.

At a point, I felt discouraged and just decided to focus on passing rather than learning.

I still get imposter syndrome - regular bouts of it; feeling inadequate and unqualified to pursue a career in tech.

But I am here to see how far I can push myself and grow in my love for technology, as there are so many things I am intrigued by and want to be able to learn how to do.

I believe that with the right amount of determination and guidance, I can go very far.

WHAT DO I HOPE TO ACHIEVE AT ALT SCHOOL?

At the end of my Alt School year, I hope to become proficient in my tech skills. I hope to connect with people in different tech spaces, as well.

Generally, I hope I am able to begin the step in my journey to becoming a front-end developer and even developing my skills further so as to go full-stack.
